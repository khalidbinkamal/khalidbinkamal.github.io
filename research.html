Current Projects

Wrongful Discharge Laws & Employee Productivity

Core question:
Do employment protection doctrines improve or reduce firm productivity?

What I am doing:
This project estimates the causal impact of wrongful discharge laws on firm-level productivity outcomes using panel data on U.S. public firms. The study evaluates competing theoretical predictions about employment protection and labor market efficiency.

Methods: Difference-in-Differences, fixed effects panel models, entropy balancing robustness checks.

Keywords: labor regulation, employment protection, productivity, causal inference, labor economics.

Status: Paper prepared for conference presentation (MPSA 2026, Chicago).

Employer-Sponsored Childcare & Employee Retention

Core question:
Does access to employer-provided childcare reduce employee turnover?

What I am doing:
This project examines the causal effect of workplace childcare provision on employee retention using quasi-experimental policy thresholds that generate exogenous variation in eligibility.

Methods: Fuzzy Regression Discontinuity Design, local treatment effects estimation.

Keywords: labor markets, gender economics, workplace policy, retention, causal inference.

Status: Accepted for presentation at the Western Social Science Association Conference (2026, U.S.).

Shelby County v. Holder and Fiscal Equity

Core question:
How did the removal of federal preclearance requirements affect distributive policy outcomes?

What I am doing:
This study evaluates whether the 2013 Supreme Court decision altered state-level public spending patterns, focusing on education, healthcare, and welfare allocations.

Methods: Difference-in-Differences, event-study models, state-year panel analysis.

Keywords: voting rights, federalism, redistribution, public finance, institutional change.

Reducing Waste of Scarce Clinical Resources: AI Prediction of Patient No-Shows

Core question:
Can predictive analytics improve healthcare resource allocation efficiency?

What I am doing:
This project analyzes whether statistical prediction of appointment no-shows can reduce wasted clinical resources and improve scheduling efficiency. The study treats missed appointments as an economic allocation problem rather than purely behavioral non-compliance.

Methods: Logistic prediction models, simulation of targeted interventions, cost-benefit analysis.

Keywords: health economics, predictive analytics, resource allocation, applied microeconomics.

Status: Ongoing project (2026).

Impact of E-Wallet Usage on Cashless Purchase Decisions

Core question:
Does digital wallet adoption causally increase cashless spending behavior?

What I am doing:
This project evaluates the causal effect of mobile e-wallet usage on digital purchasing behavior using a randomized encouragement field experiment conducted in Khulna, Bangladesh. Individuals randomly offered assistance installing a payment app are compared to a control group to identify behavioral changes attributable to adoption.

Methods: Randomized Encouragement Design, instrumental variables estimation, two-stage least squares.

Keywords: digital finance, financial inclusion, technology adoption, field experiments.

Status: Working paper.
